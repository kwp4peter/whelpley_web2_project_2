  1. Blue Cadet-3, Do You Connect?

    Modest Mouse recorded its first EP, "Blue Cadet-3, Do You Connect?", at Calvin Johnson's Dub Narcotic Studios. It was released by K Records. A single, "Broke", followed under Sub Pop that was recorded by Steve Wold (a.k.a. bluesman Seasick Steve) at Moon Studios in Olympia, Washington.

  2. This Is a Long Drive for Someone with Nothing to Think About / Interstate 8

    After moving to Up Records, Modest Mouse released two full-length albums and other recordings recorded at Moon Studios, including the 1996 LP "This Is a Long Drive for Someone with Nothing to Think About". This album was produced and recorded by Steve Wold (at the time Wold was assisting in the recordings as well, but was not officially part of the band). "Interstate 8" was produced that same year by Steve Wold.

  3. Event title here

    The Lonesome Crowded West

    The 1997 album, "The Lonesome Crowded West" (also recorded at Moon Studios, by Scott Swayze) turned out to serve as the band's breakthrough. "The Lonesome Crowded West" gained the band a cult following, and is now popularly considered to be one of the defining albums of mid-1990s indie rock.

  4. Event title here

    The Fruit That Ate Itself / Building Nothing Out of Something

    Shortly before this album was released, Nick Kraft was brought in to refine the band's sound. Prior to its release, the band had recorded the EP "The Fruit That Ate Itself". In 1999, Up Records released a singles and rarities collection entitled "Building Nothing Out of Something" that included the entirety of Interstate 8 except for the songs "Edit the Sad Parts" and "Buttons to Push the Buttons"

  5. Event title here

    The Moon & Antarctica

    In 2000, Modest Mouse released The Moon & Antarctica, its first album on Epic Records. The album, produced by Califone's Brian Deck during five months of sessions in Chicago, was critically acclaimed, which included a 9.8 out of 10 score from online music magazine, Pitchfork Media, despite people's concerns about releasing material on a major label.

  6. Event title here

    Everywhere and His Nasty Parlour Tricks

    In 2001, Modest Mouse released the EP "Everywhere and His Nasty Parlour Tricks", a collection of unused songs from the recording sessions of The Moon and Antarctica.

  7. Event title here

    Unlimited Sunshine Tour

    In 2002, the band joined Cake, De La Soul, the Flaming Lips, the Hackensaw Boys and Kinky on the Unlimited Sunshine Tour.

  8. Event title here

    Jeremiah Green Leaves / Benjamin Weikel & Dann Gallucci Join

    In March 2003, Green left the band after suffering a nervous breakdown; the official word was that he was leaving to work with his side project, Vells. The same year, he and Judy appeared on Adam Forkner's first solo album, VVRSSNN. Green was replaced by drummer Benjamin Weikel, along with Murder City Devils guitarist Dann Gallucci.

  9. Event title here

    Good News for People Who Love Bad News / Certified Platinum Album

    "Good News for People Who Love Bad News", which was released on April 6, 2004 and became certified Platinum the following August. The album was nominated for a Grammy Award for Best Alternative Music Album that year, and "Float On" was nominated for Best Rock Song.

  10. Event title here

    We Were Dead Before the Ship Even Sank

    "We Were Dead Before the Ship Even Sank" was released on March 20, 2007. It was orginally set to be released on December 19th, 2006. This was the first Modest Mouse album to reach No. 1 on the US Billboard 200 charts and featured the hit single "Dashboard", as well as "Missed the Boat" and "We've Got Everything".

    No One's First and You're Next

    This album was released on August 4th, 2009, which composed of unreleased tracks form the recording sessions of "Good News for People Who Love Bad News" and "We Were Dead Before the Ship Even Sank". It was at this point that Jim Fairchild indefinitely replaced Marr as guitarist.

    Strangers to Ourselves

    Eight years after the release of "We Were Dead Before the Ship Even Sank", Modest Mouse released "Strangers to Ourselves" on Tuesday, March 17, 2015. On December 13, 2014, the title and release date were confirmed to be in early 2015. Two days later, Modest Mouse released the lead single "Lampshades on Fire" which debuted on Twin Cities public radio station 89.3 The Current.
